Navigating a career in tech

The panel will present 4 women speakers and their diverse journeys in science and technology across domains of research, innovation, and entrepreneurship.

Sowmya Vijayalakshmi

Sowmya holds a Bachelor degree in Computer Science & Engineering and an executive leadership from IIM Bangalore. Being a gold medallist in her undergrad , she is a tech enthusiast and a passionate leader . She believes in innovation, diversity. She has worked in various organizations like WIPRO , IBM , ANZ and CISCO before joining VMware . She has been working on technologies like virtualization , operating system, networking , cloud. She is a member of the Women in Management and Women in Technology initiatives across India. She is a seasoned leader and management professional who believes people / team are the core for every success in any organization. She is passionate to work with university students , grads and help them enable the transition from college to corporate through various engagements and initiatives.

Supriya Dey

Supriya graduated with a Masters by Research from IIIT Bangalore in 2017 in IT and Society. She had an industry experience of more than two decades before she joined this course. She is the co-founder of Vision Empower, a not for profit incubated at the IIITB Innovation Center in 2017 to make STEM education accessible for students with visual impairment. She is also the co-founder of Vembi Technologies Pvt. Ltd which creates assistive technologies for inclusive education

Srujana Merugu

Srujana is an ML researcher currently working as a principal scientist at Amazon. She has a keen interest in recommendation systems, conversational AI, modeling social dynamics/human behavior, and problems at the intersection of ML and healthcare/education. Prior to her current stint, she spent time with Google research India, Flipkart, IBM Research, Yahoo research, as well as startups and non-profits (EkStep and Wadhwani AI) working with government agencies. She has published her research in several top-tier conferences and journals on machine learning and is the recipient of multiple best paper awards. She received her M.S. and Ph.D. from the University of Texas at Austin and her B. Tech. degree from IIT Madras.

The objective of the panel is to encourage intriguing discussions on opportunities and challenges in these domains. We believe that the session will help students better navigate their career paths by learning through each of the speakers' unique experiences.

Data Localization and its effect on Businesses

Privacy and information security concerns have prompted governments and regulators around the world, including India to enact data localization measures. The extent of data localization varies from free flow of data, to stringent restrictions requiring all data to be stored only within the territorial jurisdiction of the country. The Reserve Bank of India mandates all the financial transactions of the residents of India be stored in servers located only in India. It is in this context that the panel will explore the effect of data localization measures on business - both large and start-ups, consumers and society at large.
